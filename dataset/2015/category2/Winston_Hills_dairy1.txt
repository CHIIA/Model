LP  Food manufacturer and processor Eastern Investment Holdings will be busy milking sales after acquiring a  dairy

processing facility in Winston Hills for $8.6  million .

The predominantly  Chinese -owned  company  will process  dairy  products on the  site  in Sydney's western
suburbs and distribute it to major supermarkets locally and overseas. The building is spread over 6918 square
metres of space and consists of an office, large cool room, food processing area and dry goods warehouse on a
18,418sqm  site , and backs on to the M2 and M7 motorways. LJ Hooker's Marcel Elias inked the deal.

TD  Motoring ahead

A car yard  site  in Homebush, Sydney, will be transformed into an  apartment  complex following its  sale  for $12.6
million . The 2216sqm  property  on Parramatta Road was  sold  off-market to a local developer. The seller had
merged three car yard sites and obtained development approval for 84 apartments. The  site  is close to Homebush
Railway Station, the M4 motorway, schools and shopping centres. Ray White's Peter Kotzias negotiated the  sale  on
behalf of a local private  company .

$4.5m for Darlinghurst icon

The Darlinghurst building that once housed the legendary Fez Cafe has been  sold  to a private local buyer for $4.5
million . The 450sqm building sits on the corner of Victoria and Liverpool Streets and is home to Spanish restaurant
El Beso of Spain, a cafe by the name of The Bunker and a four-bedroom residence over two levels. Together, the
tenants pay a net annual rent of $265,000.

Metro  Commercial 's Allan Levy and Jerry Leis  sold  the  property  for a local private seller.

Hot auction for pharmacy

Two-thirds of a pharmacy in the Burwood Heights Shopping Centre have been  sold  to two separate foreign
investors for $2.5  million .

A  Chinese  investor and a Vietnamese investor beat more than 50 people at a heated auction and coughed up $1.2
million  and $1.3  million  each. The shops were  sold  subject to new six-year leases with six-year options at a
$66,282 net annual rent, which translates to 5.3 per cent and 5.5 per cent yields for each shop.

The shops are leased to one tenant, Quality Pharmacy, which operates 12 stores throughout Victoria. The pharmacy
at Burwood Heights is spread across three shops, two of which were  sold  at the auction on behalf of a local private
investor. The third is owned by a different investor. Teska Carson's Tom Maule and Michael Taylor were the selling
agents.

Lighting product distributor Light Application has snapped up warehouse and office facility Osborne  Park  in Perth's
north for $1.7  million .

The  property  comprises 236sqm over two levels, and 366sqm of warehouse space. Light Application will relocate
from its Balcatta  site  and occupy the facility from April 20.

CBRE's Daniel Sanzone negotiated the deal for a local private investor.

Cabramatta townhouses

A vacant piece of land in Cabramatta could host more than 40 townhouses after the  sale  of the block for $8.4
million . Melbourne-based  company  Gas Motors has  sold  the block to a local developer. Colliers International's
Harry Bui negotiated the deal on behalf of Gas Motors.
