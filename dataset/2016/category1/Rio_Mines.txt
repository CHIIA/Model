China bid for Rio mines

news in brief

A Chinese miner is looking at Rio Tinto's Australian coalmines, which could be worth $1.5 billion,
according to Investec analysts. The Australian reported that Yancoal, 78 per cent-owned by China's
Yanzhou Coal Mining, is looking at Rio's Hunter Valley and Mount Thorley Warkworth operations in
New South Wales and the Hail Creek and Kestrel mines in Queensland, all of which have been put up
for sale.
