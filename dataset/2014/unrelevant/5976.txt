AUTO REGULATOR ORDERS NATIONWIDE RECALL OF AIR BAGS
U.S. auto safety regulators, two days ahead of another round of congressional hearings, called for
recalls of millions of additional air bags by Japanese supplier Takata Corp. and several big auto
makers. (published 0129 GMT)

MAERSK LINE SEEKS TO CUT UNPROFITABLE CONTRACTS
Maersk Line, the world's biggest container-shipping company by capacity, plans to cut unprofitable
long-term contracts and raise its shipping rate again as weak demand and persistent overcapacity are
likely to continue for at least two more years, a senior official at the company said. (published 0124
GMT)
SAMSUNG UNITS SCRAP MERGER PLAN
The Samsung conglomerate's shipbuilding and engineering units said Wednesday they have scrapped
a plan to merge after facing a hefty bill to buy back shares from shareholders opposed to the deal.
(published 0226 GMT)

VIRGIN AUSTRALIA SIGNALS RETURN TO PROFIT
Virgin Australia Ltd. said it expects to return to profit in its fiscal second quarter, as jet fuel costs fall
and a bloody battle with Qantas Airways Ltd. for domestic customers eases. (published 0123 GMT)
IKEA FACES QUESTIONS OVER PRICING AS RETAILER PREPARES TO ENTER KOREA
Sweden's most famous purveyor of furniture products bearing funky umlaut-accented names is about
to make a splash in South Korea. (published 0111 GMT)
MAPLETREE LOGISTICS TRUST COMPLETES PURCHASE OF SGD34 MLN SINGAPORE
PROPERTY
Mapletree Logistics Trust Ltd., a real-estate investment trust focused on logistics properties in Asia,
said Tuesday that it completed the purchase of a S$34-million property in Singapore. (published
0116 GMT)

CARNARVON MULLS
SALE OF THAI ASSETS AFTER BIG AUSTRALIAN OIL FIND
Carnarvon Petroleum Ltd. is open to offers for its Thailand oil-production assets, as it prepares to spin
the drillbit again at what may be one of Australia's largest oil discoveries in decades. (published 0207
GMT)

Kingsoft Corp., which develops office and Internet-security software and online games, said Tuesday
that its agreement with Xiaomi Inc. has been revised to reflect the Chinese smartphone maker's
soaring demand for Kingsoft's cloud services. (published 0238 GMT)
PARAMOUNT GROUP PRICES BIGGEST U.S. REIT IPO
Office-building landlord Paramount Group Inc. raised $2.29 billion in its initial public offering Tuesday,
becoming the largest-ever IPO by a U.S. real-estate investment trust and helping breathe life into an
anemic year for new property stocks. (published 0011 GMT)

SUNCOR PLANS TO INCREASE CAPITAL SPENDING FOR 2015
Canadian oil sands companySuncor Energy Inc. said Tuesday it plans to boost capital spending next
year above 2014 levels and produce up to 585,000 barrels of oil equivalent in 2015 despite a recent
swoon in global crude oil prices. (published 0054 GMT)
NETFLIX SETS ITS SIGHTS DOWN UNDER -- UPDATE
As it rolls out its streaming video service in Europe, Netflix Inc. is already plotting further expansion to
new territories including Australia and New Zealand. (published 0027 GMT)

PROXY ADVISER
ISS RECOMMENDS REJECTION OF MICROSOFT CEO PAY PACKAGE
An influential adviser to institutional investors is recommending shareholders vote against the pay
package for new Microsoft Corp. Chief Executive Satya Nadella in a nonbinding vote at the software
company's annual meeting in early December. (published 0213 GMT)
HSBC NAMES KEE JOO WONG AS HEAD OF PAYMENTS AND CASH MANAGEMENT IN
ASIA-PACIFIC
HSBC Holdings PLC Wednesday named Kee Joo Wong as the head of payments and cash
management operations in Asia Pacific. (published 0213 GMT)

INDONESIAN UPSTREAM
OIL, GAS REGULATOR NAMES SUNARYADI AS HEAD-SOURCE
Amien Sunaryadi will be named the head of SKKMigas, Indonesia's upstream oil and gas regulator,
Tuesday afternoon, sources said. (published 0243 GMT)
