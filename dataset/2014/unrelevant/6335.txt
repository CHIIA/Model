HARBIN BANK TO START PREMARKETING FOR $1
BILLION IPO, SOURCES SAY
China's Harbin Bank plans to start gauging investor interest Thursday for its US$1 billion Hong Kong
initial public offering, becoming the latest Chinese financial firm to seek a listing in the territory amid
concerns about rising levels of bad debt on the mainland. (published 0347 GMT)

ALIBABA TO SPEND $805
MILLION FOR 60% STAKE IN CHINAVISION MEDIA -- 2ND UPDATE
Alibaba Group Holding Ltd. said it agreed to buy a 60% stake in a Chinese television-and-film
production firm for 6.24 billion Hong Kong dollars, the e-commerce company's latest bid to compete
better with rival Chinese Internet companies. (published 0558 GMT)

BAODING TIANWEI SHARES HIT DOWNSIDE LIMIT; BOND SUSPENDED FOR SECOND DAY
Shares of Baoding Tianwei Baobian Electric Co. hit their daily downside limit Wednesday following a
company warning about a potential delisting now it has recorded two consecutive years of net loss.
(published 0319 GMT)

ASSETS OF
MT. GOX CHIEF ARE FROZEN
A federal judge in Chicago froze the U.S. assets of the chief executive of Tokyo-based Mt. Gox as part
of a customer lawsuit against the defunct bitcoin trading exchange. (published 0313 GMT)

JOS. A. BANK TO BE BOUGHT BY
MEN'S WEARHOUSE -- UPDATE 2
Jos. A. Bank Clothiers Inc. was worth far less than Men's Wearhouse Inc. when Jos. A. Bank Chairman
Robert Wildrick launched a $2.3 billion takeover offer for the company. Men's Wearhouse scoffed,
calling the offer "opportunistic" and "subject to unacceptable risks and contingencies." (published 0041
GMT)

CATHAY PACIFIC 2013 NET PROFIT JUMPS ON HIGHER AIR TRAVEL DEMAND

back of strong long-haul international air travel, and it flagged an upbeat outlook for the year. (published
0535 GMT)

SPICEJET TO ORDER 42 BOEING 737-800 JETS
SpiceJet Ltd., India's second-largest budget carrier by market share, will sign a deal Wednesday to buy
42 Boeing 737-800 Max jets, said an executive of the group that controls the airline. (published 0535
GMT)

AUSTRALIA FUNDS EYE LIQUID INVESTMENTS
Australian fund managers are eying an increasingly liquid market: water. A state-run system giving
farmers fixed allocations for river water flowing into the country's agricultural heartland is tempting
speculators to buy up those rights in anticipation of higher prices. (published 0146 GMT)

GLENCORE BID TO DEVELOP AUSTRALIAN BAUXITE MINE REJECTED
The government of Australia's resource-rich Queensland state has turned down a proposal from
Glencore Xstrata PLC to develop a big bauxite project in northeast Australia. (published 2155 GMT
Tuesday)

DISNEY MEDIA NETWORKS CO-CHAIRMAN TO RESIGN
A top Walt Disney Co. executive unexpectedly said Tuesday she will be resigning, simultaneously
adding clarity to the succession race for chief executive at the media giant and opening up one of the
most powerful jobs in the television business. (published 0007 GMT)

CHESAPEAKE ACCUSED OF UNDERPAYING GAS ROYALTIES
Pennsylvanians who embraced the natural-gas drilling boom that has swept the state are starting to
sour on one of the biggest names in the business: Chesapeake Energy Corp. (published 0025 GMT)

STRIKERS IN
CHINA SAY IBM FIRED 20
Striking workers at an International Business Machines Corp. factory said the company fired 20 of the
strikers (published 0005 GMT)
