STATS CHIPPAC CONSIDERING APPROACH FOR
PURCHASE OF SHARES IN COMPANY
Stats ChipPAC Ltd., which is majority owned by Singapore state investment firmTemasek Holdings
Pte. Ltd., said Friday it is considering an offer it has received for the purchase of all the shares in the
company. (published 0149 GMT)

CAPITALAND RAISES OFFER FOR CAPITAMALLS ASIA
CapitaLand Ltd., Southeast Asia's largest property developer by market value, said Friday that it has
raised its offer to buyout minority shareholders in its shopping mall unit. (published 0109 GMT)

CREDIT SUISSE TO PAY $2.5
BILLION IN PACT
Credit Suisse Group AG has tentatively agreed to pay almost $2.5 billion to the Justice Department
and banking regulators and is expected to plead guilty to criminal charges as part of a deal that would
mark an escalation in the U.S. crackdown on financial firms, according to people familiar with the
matter. (published 2344 GMT Thursday)

GE HEADS TO PARIS TO SAVE
ALSTOM DEAL - UPDATE
General Electric Co. is sending a top executive to Paris on Friday as it works to save a $17 billion deal
to buyAlstom SA's energy business. (published 2325 GMT Thursday)

UK ELECTRONICS RETAILERS TO
MERGE IN $6 BILLION DEAL
Electronics specialist Dixons Retail PLC and cellphone retailer Carphone Warehouse Group PLC on
Thursday unveiled an all-share merger, bringing together two of the U.K.'s largest retailers to create a
company valued at more than $6 billion. (published 2352 GMT Thursday)
WAL-MART OFFERS WEAK OUTLOOK; U.S. SALES KEEP FALLING -- 2ND UPDATE
Wal-Mart Stores Inc. reported its fifth straight decline in U.S. sales, stoking concerns among investors
that the world's largest retailer may be losing its way. (published 2352 GMT Thursday)
