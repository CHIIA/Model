Australia
* GPT Group said in an April 7 news release that it agreed to develop Samsung Electronics Australia's
new national headquarters at Sydney Olympic Park. The 13,000-square-meter campus-style building will
rise at 3 Murray Rose Ave.

* Growthpoint Properties Australia said April 9 that it exchanged contracts to acquire a modern industrial
property at 99-103 William Angliss Drive, Laverton North in Victoria, Australia, for A$19.9 million before
transaction costs.

The deal reflects an initial yield of 8.3%.

Singapore
* Frasers Centrepoint Trust said April 8 that it agreed to buy the Changi City Point mall in Singapore for
S$305.0 million.
The purchase will boost the company's mall holdings to six and increase its total assets by 14% to S$2.4
billion.
Hong Kong

* Wing Tai Holdings Ltd. unit Wing Tai Properties plans to make an investment of up to HK$710 million to
build the "most expensive" residential development in Sai Wan Ho, Hong Kong, The (Hong Kong)
Standard reported April 14.
China

* Pacific Century Premium Developments Ltd. said April 8 that a unit agreed to sell its 100% interest in
Pacific Century Place in Beijing for US$928 million.

Gaw Capital Partners' Vinter Star Ltd. is buying the office-led mixed-use complex.
* Forterra Trust said April 6 that its wholly owned subsidiary China Real Estate Opportunities Ltd. inked a
share purchase agreement to sell its interest in 100% of the issued shares in Dream Land Properties
Ltd., which owns the Beijing Logistics Park in Beijing. The share sale consideration comes to S$22.2
million.
China Real Estate Opportunities inked the agreement with CLH (53) Pte. Ltd, a wholly owned subsidiary
of Global Logistic Properties Ltd.
* Kaisa Group Holdings Ltd. said April 10 that Kaisa Group (Shenzhen) Co. Ltd. and Sino Life Insurance
Co. Ltd. agreed to form a 51/49 joint venture to develop land in Shenzhen, China.
The companies jointly won the bid for the land by offering 5.40 billion Chinese yuan.

Japan

India
* Godrej Properties Ltd. said April 7 that it struck a partnership to develop a new 100-acre residential
township project in Bengaluru, India.
Other real estate news
* Brazil Hospitality Group said April 8 that it acquired the Pergamon Hotel at Rua Frei Caneca, 80 -
Consolação in São Paulo, along with three annex sites located in the back of the hotel.

The total acquisition value is 34.0 million Brazilian reais.
