Fortescue ship buy Fortescue Metals Group plans to buy eight massive ships from China for $550
million. But the world’s fourth-largest iron ore producer said the investment will not affect its
strategy to pay down more than $7 billion in debt.
Chief executive Nev Power said the purchase of eight ore carriers, each capable of carrying
250,000- 260,000 tons of ore, would improve efficiencies at Port Hedland.

Anxiety on budget Consumer anxiety rose sharply in response to spending cuts and tax hikes
announced in the federal budget, with women, low-income earners and families hit the hardest.

The National Australia Bank consumer anxiety index lifted almost three points to 64.5 in the June
quarter – the highest level since the survey began 18 months ago.

$1m fraud charges Two directors of a failed health foods business have been charged over an
alleged $1 million fraud.

Peter David Roach, 53, the former executive director of Healthzone, which operated the Healthy Life
stores, and another of the company’s directors Ge Wu, 35, have been charged over an alleged $1
million loan from the company to Mr Roach in 2011. ASIC alleges the two men conspired together
to obtain the loan for Roach.

Cancer meds patentAustralian drug producer Prima BioMed has been granted a US patent for its
ovarian cancer therapy. The company, which already holds patents for the treatment, CVac, in
Australia, Japan, Canada and Europe, will hold its US patent until 2022. The treatment is in phase
two of clinical trials.
