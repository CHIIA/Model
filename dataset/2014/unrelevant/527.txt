MCDONALD'S FACES SHORTAGE OF BEEF, CHICKEN AT SOME CHINA STORES
McDonald's Corp. is facing a shortage of products at some of its stores in China following problems
with an embattled Shanghai meat supplier that allegedly sold expired meat to fast-food chains across
the country. (published 0431 GMT)

HOSPIRA MAY
BUYDANONE MEDICAL-NUTRITION UNIT FOR $5 BILLION
Hospira Inc. has emerged as a bidder for Danone SA's medical-nutrition unit in a deal that could be
worth about $5 billion and mark the latest in a flurry of so-called inversion deals designed to sidestep
U.S. taxes, according to a person familiar with the matter. (published 0001 GMT)
JD.COM, TENCENT TO PRESELL MICROSOFT'S XBOX IN CHINA
Tencent Holdings Ltd. will use its alliance with online retailer JD.com Inc. to sell in advance Microsoft
Corp.'s Xbox One consoles in China, as it ramps up efforts to compete with electronic-commerce giant
Alibaba Group Holding Ltd. in China's online-shopping market. (published 0107 GMT)
LEIGHTON HOLDINGS' PROFIT FALLS 20%--UPDATE
Leighton Holdings Ltd.'s first-half net profit fell 20%, though the company signaled progress in
weathering a global-mining slowdown by sharpening focus on major construction contracts, including
airports and Asian casinos. (published 0142 GMT)

NAB TO SELL PARCEL OF U.K.
COMMERCIAL REAL-ESTATE LOANS -- UPDATE
National Australia Bank Ltd. has agreed to sell a GBP625 million basket of mainly distressed U.K.
loans to private-equity firm Cerberus Global Investors, further distancing the Australian bank from a
business that has weighed on it for years. (published 0114 GMT)
BOART LONGYEAR CONFIDENT IT CAN AVOID BREACHING LOAN COVENANTS
Boart Longyear Ltd. said it aimed to complete a strategic review aimed at overhauling its balance
sheet before the end of this year, a timetable that would keep the drill-rig supplier from breaching
financial covenants set by its lenders. (published 0034 GMT)
RYANAIR 1Q NET PROFIT SOARS BY 152% TO $265 MLN
Ryanair Holdings PLC, Europe's largest discount airline, said its first-quarter profit soared by 152%, as
it raised its full-year profit forecast and promised a special dividend following a year marred by surprise
profit warnings. (published 0500 GMT)

Education services provider Navitas Ltd. said its annual profit fell 31% to 51.6 million Australian dollars
