Australia
* DEXUS Property Group is said to be in talks with builder Built Holdings to transform Elizabeth Street
201-217 in Sydney into an apartment tower, The Australian reported March 29.

TD

DEXUS co-owns the building with the Perron Group.

Singapore

* Mapletree Industrial Trust's manager said March 21 that it will develop a build-to-suit facility at its
existing Telok Blangah Cluster in Singapore.

The facility, which will be built for Hewlett-Packard Singapore (Pvt.) Ltd., will be worth S$250 million.
China

* Franshion Properties (China) Ltd. said March 25 that a consortium led by its unit Shanghai Tuoying won
a bid for the land-use right of a parcel of land at X91 Hexi District in the Beijing Economic-Technological
Development Area.
The land grant premium is 3.10 billion Chinese yuan and the listing-for-sale was organized by the Land
Reserve Centre.

Japan

* Ascott Residence Trust said March 21 that it paid ¥6.3 billion for the Infini Garden rental housing
property in Fukuoka. The deal reflects an EBITDA yield of 6.6%.
* Japan Retail Fund Investment Corp. said March 24 that it completed the sale of AEON Town Ogaki in
Ogaki-shi, for ¥3.43 billion.

The buyer is Sumitomo Mitsui Finance and Leasing Co. Ltd.
* Nomura Real Estate Office Fund Inc. said March 25 that it decided to acquire the Kojimachi Millennium
Garden office and residential complex for ¥26.50 billion.
The property is being sold by Mori Trust Co. Ltd.
* Japan Retail Fund Investment Corp. said March 25 that it decided to purchase the G-Bldg. Shibuya 01
building in Shibuya-ku, Tokyo, for ¥3.23 billion from Mitsubishi Corp. Urban Development Inc.
The company will fund the acquisition from proceeds generated from the sale of four properties, namely
G Dining Sapporo, G-Bldg. Jingumae 04, G-Bldg. Jingumae 05 and AEON Town Ogaki.

* United Urban Investment Corp. said March 28 that it acquired the Marubeni Corp. Tokyo Head Office
Mita Annex, which has been renamed Shiba 520 Building, in Minato-ku, Tokyo. The transaction value is
¥2.10 billion.

* Japan Real Estate Investment Corp. said March 27 that it will acquire the Otemachi Financial City North
Tower in Tokyo from Mitsubishi Estate Co. Ltd. and Sankei Building Co. Ltd. The transaction value is
¥15.46 billion.
The company will fund the purchase with loans and own funds.

India

* Godrej Properties Ltd. said March 12 that it agreed to develop a premium housing project in Vikhroli,
Mumbai, which will have a potential saleable area of up to 800,000 square feet. Under an agreement with
Godrej & Boyce, the company will act as development manager and avail 10% of the total revenue
generated from the project as a service fee.
* On March 26, Godrej Properties said it inked a partnership to develop a residential project across 43
acres in Mundhwa, Keshavnagar in Pune. The scheme will offer about 260,000 square meters of saleable
area and will be developed as a residential development consisting of one-, two-, three- and
four-bedroom apartments.

Thailand
* Sansiri Public Company Ltd. said March 27 that it launched two new developments, the NYX by Sansiri
condominium project and the Burasari Phaholyotin-Saimai single-home project.
