SAMSUNG TO LAUNCH TIZEN SMARTPHONE IN THIRD QUARTER
Samsung Electronics Co. said Monday that it would start selling a smartphone running on a
homegrown operating system called Tizen in the third quarter of the year, marking a major step
forward in the Korean technology giant's attempts to lessen its reliance on Google Inc.'s Android
operating system. (published 0554 GMT)

TPG TO BUY UGL'S PROPERTY SERVICES UNIT FOR A$1.2 BLN-REPORT
TPG Capital is in final negotiations with UGL Ltd. to buy its DTZ property management arm for 1.2
billion Australian dollars, the Australian Financial Review newspaper reported Monday. (published
0554 GMT)

KAROON SELLS AUSTRALIAN ASSETS TO ORIGIN FOR UP TO US$800M
Karoon Gas Australia Ltd. said Monday it agreed to sell its 40% interest in natural-gas prospects
offshore northeastern Australia to Origin Energy Ltd. for up to US$800 million. (published 2319 GMT
Sunday)

JAPAN'S DAI-ICHI LIFE INSURANCE EYES U.S.
ACQUISITION
Dai-ichi Life Insurance Co. said Monday that it is considering buying a U.S. life insurer, responding to a
report that said it was considering spending over $4.87 billion to buy Alabama-based Protective Life.
(published 2342 GMT Sunday)
SAI GLOBAL ATTRACTS NEW BID INTEREST, PUTS ITSELF UP FOR SALE
Standards-and-compliance-services provider SAI Global Ltd. put itself up for sale on Monday after a
takeover approach from Pacific Equity Partners worth up to 1.11 billion Australian dollars spurred
interest from other parties. (published 2258 GMT Sunday)

CHINA SEES CITIC LISTING AS MODEL FOR STATE-FIRM OVERHAULS
China is poised this week to complete plans for one of the world's biggest mergers of the year, a $37
billion deal that could be a blueprint for overhauling massive, inefficient state-owned enterprises.
(published 1922 GMT Sunday)
