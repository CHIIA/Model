HIGHLIGHTS Updated highlights of the AAP Finance Wire for Monday, March 10:

PERTH - Leighton holdings shares have surged more than 11 per cent as Germany's Hochtief moved to
boost its stake in the company and take control of the board. (LEIGHTON, with factbox, on file)

SYDNEY - Jobs growth in February is expected to have bounced back after a weak result in January
but not enough to bring down the unemployment rate. (LABOUR FORCE PREVIEW, with factbox, on
file)
SYDNEY - Origin Energy chairman and former beverages executive Gordon Cairns is the new
chairman of retailer David Jones. (DAVID JONES on file)
MELBOURNE - Ship builder Austal has won a $US125 million ($A138.71 million) contract to build two
vessels for an unidentified naval customer in the Middle East. (AUSTAL on file)
SYDNEY - A Queensland coal seam gas operator has received a $160 million takeover offer from a
Chinese firm. (WESTSIDE on file)

SYDNEY - Westpac has appointed a former chief executive of eBay Australia and Hoyts Cinemas to its
board. (WESTPAC on file)

By Peter Trute, AAP Senior Finance Writer
SYDNEY - Rich Chinese investors are buying up almost one-fifth of new housing built in Sydney as part
of a $5 billion annual outlay on Australian real estate that is pushing up prices and will ultimately
change the way property values are calculated. (FINEX: PROPERTY CHINA, with factbox, on file)

By Garry Shilson-Josling, AAP Economist

SYDNEY - Just as Australia's top central banker was warning that house prices can go down as well as
up, they continued going up. (FINEX: HOUSING on file)
