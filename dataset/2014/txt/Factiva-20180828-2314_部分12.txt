CBA’s positive vibe Commonwealth Bank chairman David Turner says Australia’s largest lender is
cautiously positive about the 2015 financial year. He told the bank’s AGM investment in the
resources sector had tapered off, but the fruits of investment were showing up in increased
production of iron ore and LNG.

Seven’s ad woes Seven West Media has flagged a slide in its first half profit, but chairman Kerry
Stokes is upbeat about the group’s ability to lure advertisers. The media group, which owns the
Seven television network and the West Australian newspaper, fears its firsthalf profit could fall almost
10 per cent amid a weak TV ad market.

China ore growth Iron ore miner Fortescue Metals Group is confident Chinese growth will support
the company in the future. “Our confidence in China as our primary market remains strong,” chief
executive Nev Power told its AGM yesterday.
Nickel West unsold BHP Billiton has scrapped the sale of its Western Australian Nickel West
business after failing to attract a buyer. The business was put on the market six months ago, with
BHP believed to have put a broad $500 million to $800 million valuation on it. The sale had not
been achieved on an acceptable basis, BHP said.

Dulux profit upPaint, coatings and adhesives maker DuluxGroup expects its fledgling market in
China to remain subdued, in line with general economic conditions there. Dulux yesterday reported a
healthy rise in overall annual earnings.
