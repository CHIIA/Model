HIGHLIGHTS Updated highlights of the AAP Finance Wire for Thursday, May 15:

MELBOURNE - Australia's second largest telco Optus is losing mobile customers and expects mobile
revenue to continue falling over the next 12 months. (OPTUS, with factboxes, on file)

SYDNEY - Rapid house price rises are being touted as a thing of the past by the Reserve Bank
because of tougher lending standards. (RBA HOUSES on file)

MELBOURNE - Grains marketer GrainCorp will shut down its less efficient grain storage sites as it
seeks to recover from a 43 per cent drop in half year net profit. (GRAINCORP, with factbox, on file)
MELBOURNE - Treasury Wine Estates (TWE) has rejected fresh speculation that big international
alcoholic beverage groups are set to try to acquire its assets in the United States. (TREASURY WINE
on file)

PERTH - Ratings agency Moody's expects the Australian mining sector to continue to benefit from
strong Chinese demand, despite slowing growth in the world's second largest economy. (MINERS
RATINGS on file)

SYDNEY - Westfield has enjoyed a rise in retail sales across its global shopping centre chain.
(WESTFIELD on file)

PERTH - Shares in food maker Goodman Fielder have been halted from trade as it considers a
potential takeover offer. (GOODMAN FIELDER on file)

PERTH - Victorians are using less electricity as solar power becomes more popular and the weather
proves warmer than usual, according to the state's main electricity distributor. (SP AUSNET on file)

SYDNEY - The federal government's plan to create jobs for older Australian will have to overcome
decades of entrenched habits. (JOBLESS NA on file)
PERTH - A major Roc Oil shareholder is calling on the company to let investors vote on its $800
million merger with fellow energy player Horizon Oil. (ROC OIL on file)
