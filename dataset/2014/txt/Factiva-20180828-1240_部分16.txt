$1.13 BILLION, LAST YEAR $1.07 BILLION, (10), INVESTMENT, PROPERTY. SYDNEY.

The Tieck family's wealth was built when the late Norman Tieck and business partner Harold Cornock
founded the Franklins supermarket chain in 1954. In 1979, after growing the business to 75 stores, Tieck
and Cornock sold Franklins to Hong Kong conglomerate Dairy Farm International. Norman Tieck's sons
Garry and Paul both worked at Franklins before forming investment businessGwynvill Group in the early
1980s. Today Gwynvill Group has a broad spread of investments in shares, real estate and various cash
assets. Garry's sons Sean and Stephen, and Paul's son Jason are also involved with the business.
