VICTORIA Office building sale, 22 William Street, Melbourne Swann House, a 10-storey art deco office
building in Melbourne’s CBD has been sold to a Chinese group for $21.6 million. The property, with a
net building area of 5700sq m, is 90 per cent leased, returning $1.328m per year. The 5700sq m
building fronts William Street and Flinders Lane. Eugene Wood, of Kliger Wood Real Estate, said there
had been 140 inquiries received from Asian, Middle Eastern and local investors. “From a development
perspective the building lends itself to a potential hotel or as residential which would have
unencumbered views to the south towards the Yarra and the Southbank and Crown precinct,” Mr Wood
said.

Retail lease, 6/371 Elizabeth Street, Melbourne Computer specialist Mail Order Software has leased a
new outlet in Melbourne’s CBD, taking 167sq m for five years at the old Argus newspaper building on
Elizabeth Street. Savills Australia’s Jock Thomson, who negotiated the deal with Michael Di Carlo, said
Mail Order Software was looking for a more modern premises to expand. The six-storey
beaux-arts-style building housed The Argus newspaper from 1926 to 1957.

LEASE: $150,300 net a year TENANT: Mail Order Software LESSOR: S & JG Investments AREA:
167sq m AGENT: Jock Thomson and Michael Di Carlo, Savills Aust
NSW Industrial lease, 1/511 Victoria Street, Wetherill Park, Futsal Institute has leased industrial space
in Sydney’s west with plans to open an indoor sporting centre at the property. The five-year lease was
struck at a gross annual rental of $353,100. CBRE’s Elijah Shakir said the building had a high clearance
warehouse suitable for four indoor courts. “The location and exposure was the most prominent factor in
securing the building. The catchment area will benefit from a sporting facility of this nature,” he said.
