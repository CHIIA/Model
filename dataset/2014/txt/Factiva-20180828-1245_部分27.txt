HIGHLIGHTS Highlights of the AAP Finance Wire for Tuesday, Aug 19:

PERTH - release of BHP Billiton's full year financial result (1700 AEST). (BHP BILLITON to come)

SYDNEY - Insurer QBE will sell off or float businesses in three continents, including Australia, as it
works to get back on track after another disappointing profit slide (QBE WRAP to come).
BRISBANE - A private equity firm is holding onto its stake in Dick Smith as the electronics retailer
expects profit and sales to grow in the year ahead. (DICK SMITH WRAP to come)

MELBOURNE - Insurance Australia Group's (IAG) profit soared 59 per cent and more growth was
flagged on a day struggling rival QBE announced plans to fix its balance sheet. (IAG to come)

SYDNEY - Confusion about the introduction of a $7 co-payment for GP visits appears to be putting
some people off going to the doctor and others from having pathology tests. (SONIC)
SYDNEY - Steel and mining group Arrium is targeting further earnings growth after lifting its full year
profit 83 per cent.(ARRIUM WRAP to come)

SYDNEY - Low interest rates are likely to continue for a while yet as the Reserve Bank waits to see
how Australia's current economic challenges play out. (RBA MINUTES WRAP to come)

SYDNEY - Consumers appear to have shaken off the bad news about rising unemployment and
conflicts in the Middle East and Ukraine, with confidence rebounding last week. (CONSUMERS ANZ
on file)

SYDNEY - Salmon farmer Tassal has overcome drops in fish supplies, caused by hot weather, to post
a 23 per cent net profit rise to $41.6 million. (TASSAL to come)
MELBOURNE - Oil Search increased first half net profit by 34 per cent and has forecast higher
production and sales for the remainder of the year. (OIL SEARCH to come)
MELBOURNE - Transport and logistics provider Toll Holdings expects to make savings of $40 million
to $50 million in the current financial year from previously announced restructuring initiatives (TOLL
WRAP sent)

SYDNEY - Packaging business Amcor has lifted its full year profit more than 25 per cent and expects
growth in emerging markets to help drive its earnings higher over the next year (AMCOR WRAP to
come).

SYDNEY - Investors have cheered deals struck by consumer product supplier McPherson's to
distribute a string of famous fragrances, including Gucci, Dolce and Gabbana and Hugo Boss.
(MCPHERSONS to come)

BRISBANE - Household goods maker Hills Holdings plans to acquire more security and healthcare
technology businesses after posting a healthy full year profit. (HILLS to come)

SYDNEY - More Australians are being laid to rest by funeral and cemetery operator InvoCare despite
reporting a drop in profit (INVOCARE to come).
