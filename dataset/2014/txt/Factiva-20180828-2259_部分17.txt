HIGHLIGHTS Highlights of the AAP Finance Wire for Friday, Nov 21:
PERTH - The world's largest fresh meat processor JBS will pay $1.45 billion to acquire small goods
producer Primo Group as it looks to export pork to Asia. (JBS MEATS on file)

SYDNEY - The hundreds of thousands who clamoured to get a stake in Medibank Private will almost
certainly be sitting on a tidy first day gain when the shares hit the market next week. (MEDIBANK on
file)

MELBOURNE - Shares in retailer Kathmandu have slumped after it warned half year profit will fall
despite strong sales growth. (KATHMANDU on file)

SYDNEY - Department store Myer has moved to appease frustrated investors, forecasting its first profit
growth in four years. (MYER on file)
GOLD COAST - Billabong's new executive team says the troubled surf retailer is finally starting to turn
around, but it's too early to give any profit outlook. (BILLABONG on file)
PERTH - Iron ore miner Gindalbie Metals faces a perfect storm of falling iron ore prices and a strong
Australian dollar. (GINDALBIE on file)
SYDNEY - Andrew "Twiggy" Forrest bought $11 million worth of shares in his Fortescue Metals Group
when their value hit a five-year low this week. (FORTESCUE on file)
SYDNEY - Mining states and territories led the way for economic growth last financial year.
(ECONOMY STATES on file)

SYDNEY - AVJennings says Holden's exit from local car making is hurting demand for homes in
Adelaide. (AVJENNINGS on file)
SYDNEY - Abundant supply is driving commodity prices lower but the good news is that Chinese
demand for Australian resources will continue as its economy evolves, the Reserve Bank says. (RBA
MINING on file)

MELBOURNE - Biotech Proteomics International, which expects to be trading on the Australian
Securities Exchange in mid-December, says "personalised" medicine is the way of the future.
(PROTEOMICS on file)

MELBOURNE - "Big data" provider SkyFii has made a solid debut on the Australian Securities
Exchange as the company announced a joint-venture deal to set up in Indonesia. (SKYFII )
SYDNEY - Dental company Pacific Smiles has enjoyed a stellar market debut, adding $73 million in
value. (PACIFIC SMILES on file)

SYDNEY, Nov 21 AAP - National Australia Bank's US subsidiary Great Western Bancorp lifted its full
year net profit nine per cent due to cost cutting and fewer bad debts. (NAB GREAT WESTERN on file)
