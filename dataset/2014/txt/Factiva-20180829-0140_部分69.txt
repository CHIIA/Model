The Houston office of global law firmK&L Gates LLP welcomes Randel R. Young as a partner in the
firm"s energy, infrastructure and resources practice area. Young joins K&L Gates from Jackson Walker
L.L.P.
Young has more than three decades of experience advising clients in the energy, natural resource,
electric power, and related service, manufacturing, and supply sectors. An experienced oil and gas
transaction lawyer, he represents companies in U.S. and international project development and finance
transactions; on U.S. and cross-border acquisitions and dispositions of onshore and offshore acreage,
pipelines, plants, and facilities; and in the acquisition and financing of drilling rigs, production platforms,
and other offshore facilities. In addition to counseling clients on projects throughout the United States,
Latin America, the Middle East, Africa, and the Asia Pacific region, Young also guides companies through
the challenges of investing and operating in emerging markets generally.

"We are thrilled to have Randy Young join K&L Gates," said Craig W. Budner, Administrative Partner of
K&L Gates" Houston office. "He brings a unique combination of domestic and international energy
capabilities and will be an important addition to our Houston office and growing firmwide energy,
infrastructure and resources practice area."

Young"s arrival follows the additions of several other Houston partners since the office"s launch in
February 2013, including in the firm"s commercial disputes, energy, corporate/M&A, antitrust, and
restructuring and bankruptcy practices. K&L Gates also has continued to expand its global energy,
infrastructure and resources practice area during that time, having added 15 new partners in offices that
include Perth, San Francisco, Doha, Singapore, Seattle, London, Los Angeles, Portland, and Washington,
D.C., in addition to Houston.
