MARKET SAGS AHEAD OF BUDGET ON ALMOST NOMINAL VOLUME

Our market closed down 12.4 points to 5448.4 after trading in a narrow range all day, on tiny volume
worth $2.7 billion. The $A is at 93.62US c, up about 4 points from Saturday levels.

Except for a few situations that attracted trading, there was little to encourage investment ahead of the
Budget.

Meanwhile, stocks surged in most of the Asian countries.

Also Premier Li Keqiang, on tour in Africa with a 200 strong delegation from May 4 to 11 has announced
billions of dollars of new investment in Africa, saying in part Africa lacked infrastructure, while China had
surplus capacity, with the two nations complementary to each other.

The Premier's visit is part of the World Economic Forum in Africa, May 7-9.
