LONDON--Leyshon Energy Ltd. (LEN.LN) Friday reported a much narrowed first-half pretax loss, as it
reduced exploration costs, and said it remains very active on the acquisition front and has received
strong financial support for the potential acquisitions it is working on.
For the half year ended June 30 the Chinese focused energy firm made a loss before tax of $2.87
million, compared with a loss of $8.89 million. Exploration costs were $361,057, compared with
$5.34 million a year earlier.

Shares at 0815 GMT down 5.71% at 4 pence valuing the company at 10 million pounds ($16.29
million).
