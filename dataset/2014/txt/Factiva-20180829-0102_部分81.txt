Mig33 calls in deal
The Asian social media company looking to backdoor list through Perth explorer Latin Gold has struck
a deal, worth up to $10 million, with one of the world’s biggest mobile phone makers. Silicon Valley
veteran and former Perth local Steven Goh said yesterday his company, Mig33, had secured an initial
$US2.2 million ($2.4 million) from Chinese manufacturer Foxconn, with an option of a further $US7.4
million investment on shareholder approval of the reverse takeover. It is understood the deal, which
has been brewing for three months, will see Mig33 installed on Foxconn mobile phones.

Race for AHG shares
Automotive Holdings Group’s share purchase plan has closed oversubscribed. About 2000
shareholders applied for shares worth $30 million — $20 million more than the target. All applications
will be accepted.

Garden City blossoms

Garden City Booragoon is on the way to becoming WA’s biggest shopping centre after securing
structure planning approval for its $750 million expansion. AMP Capital, which owns the 77,220sqm
centre, said the decision would allow the centre to grow to 120,000sqm, creating space for new
retailers and integrating it into the surrounding activity centre.
