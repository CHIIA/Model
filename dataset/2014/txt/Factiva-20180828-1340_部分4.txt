AUSSIE BOND YIELDS RISE, $A PLUNGES ON LACK OF INTEREST

Our market closed down 0.1 points to 5511.3 after swinging between a high of 5536 and a low of 5493.9
during the day, on low volume worth $3.3 billion. Bond yields rose 6 points to 2.47% and the $A is at
92.73US c, down about 1.25 US dollars from last evening.
The banks led the way down while resources rose mildly, perhaps on good second line China eco data,
ahead of China's GDP due out tomorrow.

The Bank of Japan left interest rates unchanged as expected by all.
*Whitehaven Coal rose 7.5c to $1.55 on 3.5m shares, also saw some hope of an improvement in price
for metallurgical coal.

million received in settlement via either a dividend or buyback.
