China's leading steelmaker and the world's third major steel producer, Baoshan Iron & Steel Co Ltd
(Baosteel), has partnered with Australia's rail freight firmAurizon Holdings Ltd to initiate an A$1.42 billion
unsolicited bid for Australian resource firmAquila Resources Ltd. Baosteel and Aurizon have proposed to
pay $3.40 in cash per stock, a 38.8% premium to Aquila's price of A$2.45 per stock at the close of 2nd
May 2014. The bid is subject to a minimum approval condition of 50% of Aquila's share. After the deal is
closed, Baosteel, which is already a 19.8% stakeholder in Aquila, will own 85% and Aurizon 15%.
Seeking to secure supplies of iron ore, coking coal and manganes, Baosteel, had bought the stake in
Aquila in a deal worth around $240 million in 2009.
